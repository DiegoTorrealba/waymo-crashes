# Waymo Crashes Project 

**Author**: Diego Torrealba

**Date Started**: 2026-06-23

**Date Last Modified**: 2026-06-23

## Goal

The objective of this project is to analyze Waymo crash data, particularly in Austin, and see how it has changed over time and compared to other U.S. cities with Waymos. 

It's important to understand the safety of Waymos, particularly when they drive on the same streets humans are driving on and are vulnerable to the same road hazards human drivers face. 

## Data Source and Explanation

I obtained the data from [Waymo Safety Impact](https://waymo.com/safety/impact/#downloads), which has the following datasets: 

- `Miles per Geo`: "Total miles driven in each location (through December 2025)"
- `Crashes with SGO identifier and group membership`: "A list of cases with outcome group and other relevant collision information (through December 2025)"
- `Collision count and comparisons to benchmarks by outcome and location`: "Aggregated by outcome and location (through December 2025)"
- `Geographic distribution of benchmark and Waymo RO miles`: "Human benchmark crash counts for different outcome levels, human vehicle miles traveled (VMT), and Waymo RO miles reported by S2 cell through December 2025. This information can be used to reproduce the dynamic benchmark adjustments."
